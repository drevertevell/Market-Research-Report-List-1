<p><p>The WLAN security market encompasses different protocols for wireless network protection. WEP is the oldest and least secure, offering minimal protection against breaches. WPA improves security with stronger encryption, but has known vulnerabilities. WPA2 further enhances security with robust AES encryption, making it the current standard for secure wireless communication. The no-encryption market refers to networks that operate without any encryption, leaving them open to potential attacks. Each type serves different user needs, balancing security levels and ease of access.</p></p>
