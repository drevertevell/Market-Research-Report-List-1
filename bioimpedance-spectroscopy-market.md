<p><p>The Bioimpedance Spectroscopy market is primarily categorized into single-frequency and multiple-frequency segments. Single-frequency bioimpedance spectroscopy utilizes one frequency to measure body composition, making it simpler and often more cost-effective. In contrast, multiple-frequency bioimpedance spectroscopy employs a range of frequencies, providing enhanced accuracy and detailed insights into various tissue types and fluid compartments. This increased depth of information makes it particularly valuable for clinical applications, research, and personalized medicine, addressing diverse health and wellness needs.</p></p>
