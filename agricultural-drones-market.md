<p><p>The agricultural drones market is characterized by two primary types: fixed-wing and rotary blade drones. Fixed-wing drones are designed for long-range flights and are ideal for covering large fields due to their efficient aerodynamics and extended flight times. In contrast, rotary blade drones, commonly referred to as quadcopters, are more maneuverable and suitable for tasks requiring precision, such as crop monitoring and targeted spraying. Both types play vital roles in enhancing agricultural productivity and data collection.</p></p>
