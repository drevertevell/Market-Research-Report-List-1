<p><p>The duodenal ulcer treatment market encompasses various therapeutic classes aimed at alleviating symptoms and promoting healing. Antibiotics eliminate H. pylori infections, while proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) reduce stomach acid production, aiding in healing. Antacids provide symptomatic relief by neutralizing stomach acid. Potassium-competitive acid blockers (P-CAB) offer an alternative mechanism for acid reduction. H2-antagonists block histamine to diminish acid secretion. Ulcer protective drugs create a barrier over the ulcer, and surgery is considered for severe cases or complications.</p></p>
